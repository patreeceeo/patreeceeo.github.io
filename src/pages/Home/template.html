Hi, I'm Patrick Canfield, programmer, artist, writer, aspiring game designer/developer. This is the website where I focus on the theory, practice and results of programming. I have a another site for my artsy stuff.

Quality, not quantity, of experience is what really matters. I happen to have both. I approach every project as if it were my own, considering all aspects of what will determine its success in both the current quarter year and quarter century, identifying and challenging assumptions, asking the questions that haven't been asked because they're "obvious." I'm most passionate about the Web and video games. Currently thinking a lot about combining my interests with multiplayer games on the Web and practical websites with mutliplayer game technology and behavior.

Yes, the domain name of this site contains the word "debaser." It's a reference to the Pixies' "debaser," and also a play on the term "code base." I think there's a pernicious tendency towards group think in the software industry: There's a widespread, psuedo-religious belief in a "right way" to do everything. One of my goals with this site is to provide the examination and critical thinking that I find lacking, thus "debasing" engineering orthodoxy and personality cults.
